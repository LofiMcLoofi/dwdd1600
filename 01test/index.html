Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, safe, and respectful use of technology and the internet, involving ethical behavior, critical thinking, and understanding your rights and responsibilities in online communities, much like being a good citizen offline, to positively engage, learn, and protect yourself and others

What is digital law?

Digital law (also called cyber law or technology law) is the branch of law that governs how people, businesses, and governments use digital technologies and the internet.

What is AUP?

AUP primarily stands for Acceptable Use Policy, which is a set of rules and guidelines that define how an organization's computer systems, network, and other technology resources should be used

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's words, ideas, data, or creative work without giving proper credit, and presenting them as if they were your own.

In simple terms, Plagiarism = copying or borrowing work and not acknowledging the original source.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any act that violates the principles of honest teaching, learning, and research, including plagiarism, cheating, fabrication, falsification, and collusion, where students use unauthorized methods or materials to gain an unfair academic advantage

what is the rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content aren't governed by a single universal law. They depend on context (legal, platform, industry) and how the content is used.
